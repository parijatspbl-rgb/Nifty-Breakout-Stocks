Trading Guide
What to know before entering a breakout trade
⚠ Read Before Trading This app uses AI estimates, not live data. Always verify prices and volumes on NSE/TradingView before placing any order. This is not financial advice.
Pre-Entry Checklist
1
Check Nifty 500 Index Trend First If the index is below its 50-day MA, even great setups fail. Only trade breakouts in a rising market. Check daily on TradingView (NSE:CNX500).
2
Sector Strength A stock breaking out in a weak sector is a red flag. Confirm the sector index (e.g., Nifty Defence, Nifty IT) is also in an uptrend.
3
Delivery Volume on NSE On NSE's Bhavcopy data, check delivery % for the breakout day. Above 50% = institutional buying. Below 30% = likely intraday noise — skip it.
4
News Catalyst Breakouts with a reason (earnings beat, order win, budget allocation) hold much better. Check MoneyControl or Economic Times for any recent announcements.
5
Don't Chase — The 5% Rule If a stock is already 5% or more above the breakout level when you spot it, skip it. The risk:reward has deteriorated.
6
Entry Timing — Avoid First 15 Minutes Never enter at 9:15 AM open. Wait for opening volatility to settle. Best entry window: 9:45–11:00 AM or EOD for positional trades.
7
Position Sizing — The 1-2% Rule Risk maximum 1–2% of your total capital on a single trade. Calculate: (Capital × 2%) ÷ (Entry − Stop Loss) = Number of shares.
8
Promoter & FII Holding Check quarterly holdings. High promoter holding (60%+) + rising FII % = strong conviction. Falling promoter holding is a warning sign.
Breakout Failure Signs
✕
Price closes back below breakout levelExit immediately. This is a failed breakout / bull trap.
✕
Volume dries up after breakout dayBreakout needs follow-through volume. If day 2 and 3 have below-avg volume, reduce position.
✕
Broad market selloffIf Nifty 50 drops 1.5%+ on breakout day, wait. Macro pressure overrides individual stock setups.
